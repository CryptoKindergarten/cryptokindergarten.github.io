TLDR- Too Long Didn't Read

When given as a response to content, means it's too long and you didn't read it. When giving someone the TLDR you are summing it up for them.

So what is TLDR? TLDR is an acronym. An acronym is a word or phrase formed from the initial letters of a longer name or phrase, usually in a way that makes it easier to remember or refer to. Acronyms are often pronounced as a single word and are typically created by taking the first letter of each word in a multi-word name or phrase. TLDR stands for Too Long, Didn't Read. To truly understand the acronym TLDR we need to carefully analyze each word in the acronym. The word Too, not to be confused with 'two' or 'to', which are both homonyms of Too. A homonym is a word that shares the same spelling and, often, the same pronunciation with another word but has a different meaning. Homonyms can be classified into two main categories

1.Homographs: These are homonyms that have the same spelling but may have different meanings and may or may not have the same pronunciation

2.Homophones: These are homonyms that have the same pronunciation but different meanings and often different spellings.

In this case the word too is a homophone of two and to. Too can have two different but similar meanings. To keep it simple, we will disregard the first meaning of too that does not apply to the acronym of TLDR, that disregarded definition would be 'in addition to'. In the case of our 'too' we will defer to definition two of two for too which is 'to an excessive degree.

The next word you must understand properly is the word long. Long can as well have various meanings particularly as a form of leverage training. For our purposes this long means, extended in length, specifically, the length of a particular writing. Long is of course subjective in this manner.
